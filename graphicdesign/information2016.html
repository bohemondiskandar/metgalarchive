HOME : THEMES > 2016 : “MANUS X MACHINA : FASHION IN AN AGE OF TECHNOLOGY”

In the world of haute couture and high fashion, the annual Met Gala stands out as a beacon of creativity, elegance, and extravagance. Each year, the event attracts celebrities, designers, and fashion enthusiasts alike, showcasing some of the most remarkable and avant-garde creations in the industry. In 2016, the Metropolitan Museum of Art's Costume Institute presented a theme that merged the realms of tradition and innovation, inviting guests to delve into the intricate relationship between craftsmanship and technology. The theme, "Manus x Machina: Fashion in an Age of Technology," offered a captivating exploration of how the two seemingly disparate worlds of handcrafted haute couture and cutting-edge technology intersect and influence one another.

The juxtaposition of "Manus" (Latin for hand) and "Machina" (Latin for machine) provided a rich and nuanced framework for designers to interpret and express their visions. At its core, the theme challenged the conventional boundaries of fashion, inviting creators to blur the lines between the handmade and the mechanized, the organic and the synthetic. It celebrated the craftsmanship and artistry inherent in traditional couture techniques while embracing the transformative power of technology in shaping the future of fashion.

The "Manus x Machina" theme prompted guests to reflect on the evolving nature of fashion in the digital age, raising questions about the role of technology in shaping our understanding of beauty, identity, and self-expression. It sparked conversations about the tension between tradition and progress, the human hand and the robotic arm, the timeless allure of couture craftsmanship and the allure of futuristic innovation.

The red carpet at the 2016 Met Gala was transformed into a dazzling showcase of sartorial creativity and technical ingenuity, with celebrities and designers alike embracing the theme with gusto. From ethereal gowns adorned with intricate hand-sewn embellishments to futuristic ensembles crafted from unconventional materials, the fashion choices on display exemplified the spectrum of interpretations inspired by "Manus x Machina."

Designers such as Karl Lagerfeld, Alexander McQueen, and Iris van Herpen presented standout pieces that exemplified the theme's fusion of tradition and technology. Lagerfeld's Chanel creations seamlessly melded traditional couture craftsmanship with modern silhouettes and technological accents, while McQueen's otherworldly designs pushed the boundaries of fashion with their innovative use of materials and construction techniques. Van Herpen's avant-garde creations, inspired by science and nature, showcased the transformative potential of technology in redefining the possibilities of fashion. Overall, the 2016 Met Gala theme "Manus x Machina: Fashion in an Age of Technology" provided a captivating exploration of the intersection between tradition and innovation in the world of fashion. By celebrating the artistry of handcrafted couture and embracing the transformative power of technology, the theme challenged perceptions, ignited imaginations, and reaffirmed the enduring relevance of fashion as a form of creative expression.
